Singer Name: Umm Kulthum

Basic Information:
Full Name: Fatima Ibrahim El-Sayed El-Beltagi
Country: Egypt
Born: December 31, 1898
Died: February 3, 1975
Genre: Arabic classical

Early Life:
Umm Kulthum was born in a rural Egyptian village. She learned religious recitation from her father, which shaped her vocal discipline and pronunciation.

Career Development:
She moved to Cairo, where she transitioned into mainstream music. She collaborated with leading composers like Mohamed Abdel Wahab and poets such as Ahmed Rami.

Performance Style:
Her concerts were legendary:
- Often lasted several hours
- Included improvisation (tarab)
- Audience interaction was very strong

Musical Characteristics:
- Exceptional vocal strength
- Ability to repeat and vary phrases emotionally
- Deep mastery of maqam

Famous Songs:
- Enta Omri
- Alf Leila Wa Leila
- Al Atlal
- Fakarouni
- Amal Hayati

Cultural and Political Role:
Her concerts were broadcast across the Arab world and became cultural events. She was also associated with Egyptian nationalism and unity.

Legacy:
She is widely regarded as the greatest Arab singer of all time, and her influence continues in music education and performance.
