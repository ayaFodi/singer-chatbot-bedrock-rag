Singer Name: Fairuz

Basic Information:
Full Name: Nouhad Wadie Haddad
Country: Lebanon
Born: November 21, 1935
Genres: Arabic classical, Lebanese folk, traditional, orchestral

Early Life and Background:
Fairuz was born in Beirut into a humble family. Her father worked in a printing press, and despite limited resources, she was exposed early to music. She joined the Lebanese Radio Chorus as a teenager, where her talent was discovered.

Career and Rise to Fame:
Her breakthrough came through her collaboration with the Rahbani Brothers (Assi and Mansour). Together, they created a completely new style of Arabic music that blended traditional melodies with theatrical storytelling and Western orchestration.

During the 1950s–1970s, Fairuz became the most prominent voice in Lebanon. Her songs were broadcast daily, especially in the mornings, becoming a ritual for millions of listeners.

Musical Style:
Fairuz is known for:
- A soft yet highly controlled voice
- Emotional restraint and subtle expression
- Integration of poetry into music
- Use of both traditional Arabic maqamat and Western musical arrangements

Themes in Music:
- Love and longing
- Nostalgia and memory
- National identity (especially Lebanon)
- Spiritual and religious themes

Notable Songs:
- Nassam Alayna El Hawa
- Zahrat Al Madaen
- Kan Enna Tahoun
- Saalouni El Nas
- Bhebbak Ya Lebnan

Cultural Impact:
Fairuz is considered a symbol of unity. During the Lebanese Civil War, her music remained neutral and was loved by all sides. Her voice is often described as “the voice of Lebanon.”

Legacy:
Fairuz remains one of the most respected artists in the Arab world. Her music is studied academically and continues to influence modern singers.
